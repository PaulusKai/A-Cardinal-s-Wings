# WELCOME!

“Namaste.”

## TLDR

We are high school seniors.

This book attempts to distill all the lessons we've learned in our years at high school. The material here embodies “what we wish someone had told us before we started high school.”

Why do we think you should read this? We think pointing out and solving some common pitfalls that high schoolers run into would prove useful in living the best high school life.

## START

Here’s what we’re imagining: you’re sitting in a chair, maybe during the registry or lunch, and you’re thinking:

> “Lol. Are they just doing it for the college app?”

No, we aren’t. There are a lot more “college efficient” activities than sitting down to write a book — tbh I think this is probably one of the most inefficient things you can do to get into a school.

> meme: they got us in the first half, not gonna lie.

Unless you’re truly exceptional, high school is a struggle: perhaps the classes could be hard, we are lonely, or we can’t seem to achieve our goals because we are addicted to scrolling/manga/YouTube. Or maybe it’s not a struggle yet. But trust us, problems are going to arise because you’re human. It's like driving through SF's 19th Avenue and trying not to have the car feel like a roller coaster.

Because there's a 99.99% chance that you could stumble in the many pitfalls — why would us seniors sit back when we can help our younger peers out?

So we are going to do what’s called a pro gamer move: reach out to our younger peers, point out pitfalls, and give our solutions/thoughts.

![We’re gonna do what’s called a pro gamer move.](Pasted%20image%2020260312135618.png)

You see, we made mistakes, and one way to turn it into something positive is to tell you.

I, Justin, learned that when I could finally stomach my ego to ask my older peers for help, I could learn way more than I could do by myself. Not only that, I realized that many of them, especially those scary seniors, are actually really kind! Some guys, like Tyler, had the aura of a sigma. I swear, you can literally hear the sigma grindset theme when those guys are striding through crowds and halls! AND they really wanted to help out!

We can pick up where others left off! You can pick up the tools we've forged over the years, use them to soar even higher than us, and when it's your turn to go, share your insights for the next generation!

---

This book is our attempt to distill all the lessons my peers and I have learned in our years at high school.

The material here embodies “what we wish someone had told us before we started high school.”

Our mission: make a lasting and positive difference for our younger peers.

Hey, we would clap our hands with delight if we learned that you have done something new and worthwhile from this book, and tried whatever "antics" we suggested.

Why do we think you should read this? We think pointing out and solving some common pitfalls that high schoolers run into would prove useful in living the best high school life.

## Disclaimer

By the way, we aren’t here to tell you that you gotta claim the throne of "The AP King/Queen" by taking the most APs in US History. Or to "achieve" a sleep schedule of an alien — that is, a nonexistent one! We won’t sing hymns on how great you are. You already know you are. This book was designed and written to be as helpful as possible to you.

The book is not a collection of stories of past achievements. Instead, I have tried to organize and generalize the lessons of various experiences to produce a set of guidelines for our younger peers.

If you see anything profound here, assume it is from the experts/teachers we learned it from. If you see anything foolish, assume it is our error.

I know that not every book is perfect, and all of them succeed in some aspects and fail in others. So IF YOU HAVE A QUESTION, ASK!! There’s no way we can read your mind and slip answers on your bed. We don't have the funds to slip your answers through your chimneys like Santa!

---

So what's the best way to have the best high school experience?

We don't know the golden formula.

But we do have some suggestions.

To that end, we offer this book.
